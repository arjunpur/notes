- Pedagogical purpose: Make Fisher information visible as local sensitivity and curvature, with uncertainty shrinking as curvature increases.

- What the reader should notice first: A sharp likelihood peak gives a narrow uncertainty interval; a flat peak gives a wide interval.
- Necessary labels: low information, high information, \(\theta_0\), uncertainty, score = slope, curvature.
- How the text references the figure: Section 17.2 points to Figure~\(\ref{fig:ch17-fisher-curvature}\) when connecting Fisher information to likelihood curvature and estimator standard error.
- Teaching caption: Fisher information is local sensitivity of the model to the parameter. A steep, sharply curved log-likelihood near the true parameter corresponds to high information and narrow uncertainty. A shallow curve corresponds to low information and wide uncertainty. The score is the slope of the single-observation log density as the parameter changes.

## ch17-testing-ci.png

- Pedagogical purpose: Connect hypothesis testing and confidence intervals as two procedures derived from sampling distributions.
- Section(s) supported: 17.3 Hypothesis testing and confidence intervals.
- Exact mathematical objects shown: Null sampling distribution of a statistic \(T\), two-sided rejection regions beginning at \(\pm z_{1-\alpha/2}\), observed statistic \(t_{\mathrm{obs}}\), mirror cutoff \(-|t_{\mathrm{obs}}|\), two-sided p-value area \(P(|T_{\mathrm{null}}|\ge |t_{\mathrm{obs}}|)\), repeated confidence intervals, true parameter \(\theta\), and one missed interval.
- What the reader should notice first: The test rejects in prespecified tail regions, but the two-sided p-value is the smaller pair of tails beyond the observed absolute statistic; interval coverage is a repeated-sampling property.
- Necessary labels: \(H_0\) sampling distribution, reject, do not reject, \(\alpha/2\), \(t_{\mathrm{obs}}\), \(-|t_{\mathrm{obs}}|\), two-sided p-value, confidence intervals, true theta, coverage.
- How the text references the figure: Section 17.3 points to Figure~\(\ref{fig:ch17-testing-ci}\) when explaining p-values, tail regions, and repeated-sample coverage.
- Teaching caption: Testing and confidence intervals are two views of sampling distributions. A hypothesis test compares the observed statistic with its null distribution and rejects in prechosen tail regions. For a two-sided test, the p-value is the sum of both null tails beyond \(\pm |t_{\mathrm{obs}}|\), not the whole prechosen \(\alpha/2\) rejection region. A confidence-interval procedure produces random intervals that cover the fixed true parameter at the advertised long-run rate.
