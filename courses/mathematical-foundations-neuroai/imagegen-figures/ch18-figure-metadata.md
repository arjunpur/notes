- Pedagogical purpose: Teach mutual information as the difference between a true joint distribution and the product of marginals.
- Section(s) supported: 18.3 Mutual information.
- Exact mathematical objects shown: A \(2\times2\) joint distribution \(p(x,y)\) over low/high stimulus and quiet/active response, marginal distributions \(p(x)\) and \(p(y)\), the product table \(p(x)p(y)\), and the formula \(I(X;Y)=D_{\mathrm{KL}}(p(x,y)\|p(x)p(y))\).
- What the reader should notice first: Diagonal structure in the joint table creates dependence that is absent from the product of marginals.
- Necessary labels: joint \(p(x,y)\), marginals, product \(p(x)p(y)\), \(I(X;Y)=D_{\mathrm{KL}}(p(x,y)\|p(x)p(y))\), 0 if independent, X, Y, low, high, quiet, active.
