\section*{Preface}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Preface}

These notes study higher brain function as a set of modeling problems: perception, attention, memory, decision making, and control are treated as latent computations inferred from behavior and neural data. The course therefore moves between three levels of explanation: what problem the organism appears to solve, what algorithm could solve it, and what neural mechanism could plausibly implement that algorithm.

The notes are intentionally mathematical but not notation-driven. Equations are included when they clarify a representational claim, a dynamical mechanism, or an inference procedure. A useful model should compress data, expose its assumptions, and make clear predictions about where it should fail.
